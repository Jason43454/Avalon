Let's remember the cats who fought for our freedom

This page is dedicated to remembering the story of the brave cats who fought for our freedom during World War II. These little heroes deserve to be remembered and honored. They contributed in their own way and paved the way to the future we have today.

Notable Cats Throughout History

Tiddles in the Victorious

Tiddles was born aboard the Argus before joining the crew of the Victorious. His favorite spot was the stern capstan, where he played with the bell rope. It traveled more than 48,000 kilometers.

Convoy on the Hermione (Gibraltar, November 26, 1941)

Convoy was the cat on the Hermione and is listed in the ship's book. He was assigned a tiny hammock and complete equipment. He died with eighty-seven other companions when they were torpedoed by a German submarine U-205 on June 16, 1942.
